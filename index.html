Born in Italy


The cappuccino’s roots trace back to 17th-century Italy, inspired by the color of the robes worn by Capuchin monks — “cappuccio” meaning “hood” in Italian. The mix of espresso, steamed milk, and foam resembled their brown cloaks, giving this beloved drink its name.

European Coffee Culture


By the early 1900s, the cappuccino became a symbol of café culture in Europe. Served in porcelain cups with a perfect balance of espresso, milk, and foam, it represented a ritual — a slow, social moment to savor flavor and connection. The invention of the espresso machine only elevated its popularity.
